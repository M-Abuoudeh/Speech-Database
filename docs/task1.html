Task Description

Part 1: Reading Task

This task involves reading "Laylaa witheeb" (ليلى والذئب) story, which is a version of the well-known tale 'Little red riding hood' written by the experimenter in Arabic script. The story was displayed on a computer screen, and participants were asked to read it aloud in their own Jordanian dialect.

Participants were instructed to adapt the story to match their dialectal preferences. If any word in the provided text did not align with how it is said in their dialect, they were encouraged to replace it with the appropriate equivalent from their local speech. This approach allowed the recordings to authentically reflect the phonetic characteristics and lexical choices of each participant's dialect.

Part 2: Storytelling Task

After completing the task of reading the story "Laylaa witheeb", participants were asked to narrate the same story without reading or referring to the original text.

Participants were instructed to recount the story as if they were telling it to a family member, using their own dialect as naturally and authentically as possible. This task aimed to capture spontaneous speech and narrative skills in their native dialect.
